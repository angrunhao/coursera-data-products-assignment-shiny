Wage dataset

With the Wage panel dataset from the ISLR package, we will attempt to explore the relationship between age and wage. Does wage increase monotonically with age, meaning simple linear regression can capture the relationship, or is there some nonlinear relationship with age?

We look at the two columns we are most interested in here

##             wage age
## 231655  75.04315  18
## 86582   70.47602  24
## 161300 130.98218  45
## 155159 154.68529  43
## 11443   75.04315  50
## 376662 127.11574  54

Graphical relationship 1

We look at the graphical plot, plotting the regression line in blue

plot(Wage$age, Wage$wage, pch = 4)
abline(lm(wage ~ age, data = Wage), col = "blue")

Graphical relationship 2

We then add various datapoints that reflect the mean wage of that particular age in red.

Comparing predictions and underlying mean

It is apparently that both at young ages and old ages, there is an overestimation of the wage predicted using simple linear regression. We can see why due to the huge overcrowding of information in the middle-aged range, and then double dip at both young and old age cannot be adequately captured with a linear regression.
